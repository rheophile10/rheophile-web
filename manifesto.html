Manifesto

Three commitments.

01

Own the software.

Water nourishes all things without trying, and asks nothing of them. Software should be the same: free to run, open to the last line, demanding no account and no permission it hasn't earned. What you can't read, you don't own — you rent.

02

Keep the data local.

On your disk, working offline, leaving only when you send it. Collaboration is trading signed deltas — merged like belts, not surrendered to a host.

03

Distrust the center.

Centralized compute and storage that isn't earned by active community merges is bad most of the time. Push the keys and the compute back to the edges.

Own it. Read it. Run it anywhere. Keep your data close, trade it freely, and be slow to trust a center that didn't earn it by merging.
